SPSMeet User Manual

Introduction

SPSMeet is a Saskatoon Public School Division interactive meeting website.  It allows teachers, students, and staff to create virtual text-based meeting rooms which anyone with the meeting name can join.  A transcript of the meeting can be viewed at any time, even after the meeting has closed.

Create Meeting

To create a meeting room:

1.        Navigate to the home page.

2.        Enter the desired meeting name.

3.        Select a closing date from the calendar.  The selected closing date will appear in the closing date field with a default time of 12:00 AM.  This date/time can be manually modified before the meeting is created*.

4.        Click submit.

 

If a meeting with this name is already in progress the input box will turn red.

 

* date/time text format: day/month/year hour:minute:second (AM or PM)

            Ex: 06/12/2011 02:15:00 PM = December 6, 2011 at 2:15 PM

Join Meeting

To join a meeting room:

1.        Navigate to the home page.

2.        Enter the name of an existing meeting.

3.        Click Submit.

Changing Themes

Themes determine the appearance of SPSMeet.

 

·           Layout – changes the location of elements in the meeting room

·           Color – determines the color scheme used for the entire site

·           Font – determines the font used for the entire site

·           Streaming – allows everyone in the meeting room to see what you type as you type it

·           Stream –  With this option set, messages will be displayed in the conversation window as they are typed.  Also, you will see streamed messages from others who have streaming on.

·           Hide – With this option set, you will not stream your message as it is typed.  Also, you will not see streamed messages from others.

 

Mobile site tested and working on:

·           iPod Touch – Safari browser

·           Android – default browser

 

Remember:   You must press submit after changing theme settings for these changes to take effect.

Conversation

·           Messages are displayed here in reverse chronological order (newest first).

·           The meeting creator has the ability to remove any message via a delete button that is attached to every message.  Everyone else in the meeting can only delete their own messages.  Deleting a message is permanent.

Write Message

·           Type your message in the text field.

·           Click ‘Send’ to share it with the rest of the meeting room.

·           If streaming is enabled, others in the meeting room who have streaming enabled will be able to see your message as it is typed.

Members

·           Lists all of the people who have joined the meeting.

·           Present members are marked with an ‘X’.

·           Absent members are marked with an ‘O’.  Absent indicates that a member has left the meeting room (i.e. closed the meeting room page).

Existing Meetings

The Existing Meetings page displays your relevant meeting information:

·         Joined Meetings – A list of open meetings that you have joined.

·         My Open Meetings – A list of open meetings that you have created.

·         My Closed Meetings – A list of closed meetings that you have created.  Closed (expired) meetings are removed after 30 days.  Specifically, the meeting creator has access to their expired meetings transcripts’ for 30 days after the expiry date.

 

 

1.         Navigate to the home page

2.         Join ‘MyMeet’ by clicking its name.  You will have to join a meeting manually from the home page before it is shown in this list.

3.         View the transcript for ‘myopenmeeting’

4.         Join ‘myopenmeeting’

5.         View the transcript for ‘myclosedmeeting’

Meeting Transcripts

Meeting transcripts provide a list of all messages sent during the course of the meeting.  Transcripts of open and closed meetings can be viewed by the meeting creator from the ‘Existing Meetings’ page.

 

Before the transcript is viewed, the creator must specify which names will be visible by selecting the corresponding checkbox.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Who has access to SPSMeet?

A: Anyone with a Saskatoon Public School Division (SPSD) Windows account.

 

Q: Who can join my meeting?

A: Anyone who has access to SPSMeet and knows the name of your meeting.

 

Q: How long are meeting transcripts kept for?

A: Thirty days after the closing date, after which they are permanently removed.
